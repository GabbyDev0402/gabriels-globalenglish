From Hesitant to Fluent: How Watching Movies Unlocked My English Potential (and Can Help You Too!)

As a Filipino, English is technically my second language. But honestly, before I started working as a Customer Service Representative, I couldn't speak or even think in English fluently. Using it functionally felt like a distant dream. I knew that needed to change, and fast. That's when I discovered the methods I now use at Gabriel's Global English to help my students. English was essential to my job and the key to my improvement was making learning fun and engaging.

My secret weapon?

English movies, TV series, and YouTube content. Immersing myself in how native speakers actually use the language, and then imitating them, was a game-changer. It even helped me to start thinking in English. At first, it was just narrating my day in my head, simple things like, "I'm going to make coffee now," or "I need to walk the dog." But then, I started imitating memorable conversations or lines from movies I loved. I'd find myself replaying scenes in my head, mimicking the characters' intonation and phrasing. It felt a little silly at first, but it was incredibly effective!

Here's the approach I found most effective:

  1. Subtitles are Your Friends (at First): I started by watching with subtitles. This wasn't just about understanding the plot; it was a fantastic way to learn new vocabulary and get familiar with the rhythm of spoken English – the pronunciation, the intonation, all of it.
  2. Rewatch and Absorb: I'd pick a movie, watch it once with subtitles, and then again without. This repetition was key. Whether it was a complex film that needed a second viewing or a comforting favorite I enjoyed watching again, rewatching helped me absorb the language on a deeper level.
  3. Make it a Habit, Make it Fun: This might sound like work, especially if you prefer watching content in your native language. But trust me, you can find something you love. Dive into detective thrillers, heartwarming dramas, laugh-out-loud comedies, or whatever sparks your interest. You'll be experiencing the world of cinema while simultaneously leveling up your English skills.
  4. Talk it Out: Find a friend or language partner (or a tutor like me!) to discuss your favorite movies with. It is like having a friendly conversation that will help you to practice. It’s a fantastic way to practice speaking, share insights, and truly solidify what you're learning. This is one of the fastest ways to boost your fluency.

Movie and Show Recommendations to Get You Started:

To help you on your English-learning journey, here are a few beginner-friendly recommendations:

Comedies:

  • Friends: A classic with easy-to-follow dialogue and relatable situations.
  • The Good Place: Philosophical but funny, with clear and engaging conversations.

Dramas:

  • To All the Boys I've Loved Before: A teen romance with simple language and a heartwarming story.

Animated:

  • Finding Nemo: Clear and engaging for all ages, with simple vocabulary and fun characters.
