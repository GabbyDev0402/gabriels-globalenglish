From CSR to Confident Communicator: My Journey to Mastering English

Before I embarked on my journey as a Customer Service Representative (CSR) two years ago, I struggled to communicate in English. As a Filipino, I only understood basic English words, but being hired as a CSR pushed me to master the language. Now, I want to share my tips with you on how to think in English and improve your fluency.

1. Watch English Movies, Videos, and Documentaries

Choose a genre that interests you and start watching movies in English. Initially, you may use subtitles, but try to gradually transition to watching without them. This helps immerse you in the language and pick up nuances in speech and context.

Evidence: According to Psychology Today, watching movies in a foreign language improves listening skills, vocabulary, and comprehension.

2. Listen to English Music, Audiobooks, and Podcasts

Fill your idle moments with English voices by listening to music, audiobooks, and podcasts. Repeating words and phrases as you hear them helps reinforce your learning and makes it a habitual practice. This constant exposure will train your brain to think in English more naturally.

Evidence: Educational Technology and Mobile Learning suggests that listening to English content regularly can significantly improve your language skills.

3. Speak in English, Even If It Feels Uncomfortable

Practice speaking English even during routine activities like doing laundry, cleaning the house, or cooking. Use simple English phrases to start, and gradually build your confidence. You can also imitate scenes from movies or sing along to English songs.

Evidence: The British Council recommends speaking practice as an essential part of improving fluency.

4. Keep a Journal and Practice Freewriting in English

Maintaining a journal and practicing freewriting in English can significantly enhance your writing skills. If you find it challenging, start with writing prompts and gradually increase your writing length.

Evidence: The Journal of Second Language Writing highlights that regular writing practice helps in developing better writing skills and enhances overall language proficiency.

Making English a habit is the key. Do not aim for perfection; rather, practice consistently and keep trying!
